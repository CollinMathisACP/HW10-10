Time Series

Fundamental Difference

Chapter 7 deals with distributions. In this form of data, there are multiple data points of a single variable, each with their own value. The meaning of this data is found primarily in the frequency of each value, along with other factors that are consequences of that primary focus. In chapter 8, the data in question comes in the form of a time series. This is a single continuous variable that changes over a period of time. The fundamental difference between these two data types is the former finds meaning in frequency, and the latter in progression over time.

Time Series Line Charts

Pros

  • Presents a clear visualization of the variable
  • Larger trends in the data are easy to identify

Cons

  • Factors such as seasonality and noise can muddle the data without it being obvious
  • Can require substantial transformation to identify more subtle trends

Logarithmic Scales

Pros

  • Can reveal a trend in exponential data
  • Results in a scale that is easier to make sense of

Cons

  • Is highly situational, requires exponential data

Horizon Charts

Pros

  • Elegant, can give an overview of many related variables at once
  • Visually appealing, even to someone with minimal understanding of statistics

Cons

  • Can be hard to focus in on a single instance
